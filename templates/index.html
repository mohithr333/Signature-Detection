ENHANCED HANDWRITTEN SIGNATURE RECOGNITION WITH CNN-SVM FUSION

ABSTRACT

Handwritten signature recognition plays a crucial role in domains like security, document authentication, and identity verification. This project presents an innovative approach that integrates Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) and Support Vector Machines (SVM) to achieve precise and efficient signature recognition. The project's primary objective is to develop a system capable of training on user signatures and subsequently identifying individuals based on their unique handwritten patterns.

In the training phase, our system utilizes a dataset of user signatures to learn and extract distinctive features from each individual's writing style. The CNN is employed to automatically extract pertinent features from signature images, resulting in robust representations for subsequent classification. Each signature is linked to its respective user, forming a comprehensive reference database.

During the testing phase, we employ a user-friendly web interface to capture input images of user signatures. Our system then leverages the trained CNN and SVM models to classify these signature samples. By comparing the input signature against the stored database, the system determines its authenticity. The classification result is promptly presented to the user, providing a convenient and reliable means of verifying handwritten signatures.

Our proposed system demonstrates the potential to significantly enhance signature recognition accuracy and security across various applications, including document verification and access control. The combination of CNN and SVM algorithms establishes a robust framework for both training and testing phases, ensuring the system's effectiveness in real-world scenarios.
